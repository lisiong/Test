Samsung Flip Basics

1. About Samsung Flip

Samsung Flip is an interactive digital flipchart for team collaboration such as brain storming and note-taking activities

  • Connectivity for USB, laptop, mobile devices
  • Smooth writing plus UHD picture quality

The Samsung Flips are currently set up at SR1Q.

2. Quick Start

To power up the Flip, push the Power button.

3. Writing with the Flip

a. Drawing / Erasing

There are 2 tips on the supplied pen for writing on the Flip.

b. Changing pen thickness

You can change the pen thickness at Settings → Pen → Pen & Highlighter Thickness

c. Changing the pen colour

You can change the pen colour by touching and holding the screen with the pen.

d. Erasing

You can use your finger or palm to erase your writing.

  • Use your finger for fine erasing
  • Use your palm to erase an area

4. Presenting with the Flip

A 'roll' is a file with multiple pages (up to 20 pages) linked together.

Pages are linked vertically in portrait orientation, and horizontally in landscape orientation.

a. Seamless scroll

Hold and drag the sides of the screen to scroll through the roll.

b. Pivot / Rotate

Showcase content in portrait or landscape mode by rotating the screen

6. Connect & Share

Import / Synchronise - Mirror materials directly from your mobile devices onto the screen

a. Connecting to a Laptop using a cable

b. Connecting to a Laptop using WiFi Display

c. Connecting to a Mobile Device using Smart View or Screen Mirroring

  1. Drag down the top bar on the mobile device screen to start Smart View or Screen Mirroring.
  2. Select this product from the list of devices displayed on the screen.

d. Importing from a USB Device

e. Export created rolls to a USB Device

The Flip saves files in two versions (IWB and PDF formats).

Connect an external HDD or a USB storage device.

7. Interacting with imported content

a. Source Window

When you connect a laptop or a mobile device to the product, the Source Window appears.

b. Document Viewer

When you open a document file (DOC, PPT, PDF), the Document Viewer displays the document file you select to import from a USB device or network drive.
